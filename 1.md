# CONTROL F — DEMO APP MASTER SPEC
# Everything you need to build this app from scratch

---

## WHAT IS THIS

A demo app for Control F (controlf.ai), an AI-powered monitoring tool that automatically extracts financial metrics from US public pension fund documents and alerts users when data changes. We are building this to demonstrate our technical capabilities to the Control F founders (Giulio Comellini and Juan Agustin Tibaldo) so they bring us onto the team.

The demo uses REAL data extracted from 4 sample documents they provided us. Everything is pre-loaded — no backend needed, no live API calls required (though we can add manual upload with Claude API later).

---

## BRANDING

- Name: **Control F**
- Tagline: "AI-powered intelligence across US public pension fund documents"
- Logo: "CONTROL F" text with a stylized F (or just the text for now)
- Domain reference: controlf.ai

---

## THE USER

Fund-of-funds analysts, GP capital raising teams, and investment consultants who need to track which pension funds are committing capital to which funds, at what terms, and when managers get hired/fired. They currently do this by manually reading hundreds of PDFs from pension fund websites. Control F automates that.

### Sarah — Primary Persona

Sarah works at a fund-of-funds in New York. Her job is tracking infrastructure fund commitments across US pension funds so her team knows who's raising capital, who's allocating, and where the money is flowing.

**Her day right now:**
She opens 15 browser tabs. ISBI. PSERS. Minnesota SBI. CalPERS. NJ Treasury. She clicks through to "meeting minutes" or "board materials." She looks for new PDFs she hasn't read yet. She downloads three. She opens the first — 23 pages. She CTRL+F "infrastructure." She CTRL+F "commitment." She finds a paragraph buried on page 7 that says someone committed $150M to some fund. She copies that into her Excel tracker. She does this for the next PDF. 45 minutes gone. She probably missed something in the DCRB minutes because the fund name was redacted as "Fund AN" and she didn't realize it was infrastructure.

**Her day with Control F:**
She opens the app. There's a notification: "3 new documents detected. 8 new metrics found since your last scan." She clicks in. She sees: DCRB committed $150M to Fund AN (infrastructure). NJ DOI proposing $300M to Ardian. Santa Barbara committed $7.5M to NOVA Infrastructure Fund II. Every single number has the source quote right there — she clicks and sees the exact sentence from the document. She trusts it because she can verify in 2 seconds.

Done. 45 minutes → 2 minutes. And she didn't miss the "Fund AN" thing.

**What matters to Sarah — every feature decision filters through these:**
1. **Don't make me check websites.** Check them for me.
2. **Show me WHAT CHANGED since last time.** Not everything — just what's new.
3. **Let me verify instantly.** Show me the exact text from the document.
4. **Don't get it wrong.** One wrong number and I'll never trust you again.

### Competitive Analysis

**What Control F (the current product) has right:**
- The evidence panel is their best feature
- The entity selector organized by state is smart
- The email digest is what Sarah actually wants in her inbox

**What Control F does poorly:**
- The evidence text looks like a raw dump — monospace, ugly, feels like a terminal
- The results table is flat and lifeless
- There's no "what changed" view — just a list
- No aggregate picture

**What we do better:**
- The evidence panel should feel like you're reading the actual document — clean typography, the key value highlighted with a colored background, surrounding context visible. Not a code block.
- The "what changed" moment should be front and center. When Sarah opens the app, the FIRST thing she sees is alerts: "New: DCRB committed $150M to infrastructure. New: NJ DOI proposing $300M to Ardian." Not buried in a table.
- The part only we can build: the agent that actually goes to websites, finds new PDFs, downloads them, and extracts accurately. That's the engine. Without that, it's just a pretty UI with no data.

**The demo story:** Not "look at my UI." It's: "I built the engine that feeds your product. And I made the evidence look trustworthy."

## THE FEEL

Dark mode. Premium fintech. Bloomberg Terminal meets ChatGPT meets Linear. Data-dense but clean. Smooth animations between every state transition. The processing/loading moments should feel like intelligence at work, not like waiting. Everything should look significantly better than the current Control F product (reference their screenshots — functional but basic).

## APP STRUCTURE

Single page app with sidebar navigation. Five sections:

1. **Search** — the entry point
2. **Results** — extracted metrics table with evidence
3. **Dashboard** — charts and aggregate intelligence
4. **Trackers** — saved monitoring queries
5. **Upload** — manual PDF upload for one-off extraction

For the demo, Search → Results is the hero flow. Dashboard, Trackers, and Upload are secondary but should look complete and populated.

---

## DETAILED UX FLOW

### SEARCH (initial state)

What the user sees:
- Sidebar on the left: Search (active), Results, Dashboard, Trackers, Upload
- Center of the page: the Control F logo and tagline
- A large search bar below, Google/ChatGPT style
- Placeholder text: "What do you want to track?"
- Below the search bar, clickable preset pills:
  - "New LP commitments to infrastructure funds"
  - "Manager terminations and replacements"
  - "Private equity fund performance (IRR/TVPI/DPI)"
  - "New funds registered in the SEC"

What happens:
- User clicks a preset OR types a query and hits enter
- The search bar animates up to the top of the page
- Presets fade out
- Transition to PROCESSING state

### PROCESSING (3-4 seconds)

What the user sees:
- Search bar is now pinned at the top
- Center of the page: an animated visualization suggesting AI scanning documents
- Status messages cycling with fade transitions every ~1.5 seconds:
  1. "Scanning NY State Common Retirement Fund..."
  2. "Analyzing NJ Division of Investment filings..."
  3. "Reading DC Retirement Board minutes..."
  4. "Extracting data from Santa Barbara County ERS..."
  5. "Cross-referencing fund commitments..."
  6. "Building intelligence signals..."
- A small counter incrementing: "documents scanned... metrics found..."

What happens:
- After 3-4 seconds, smoothly transition to RESULTS state
- The processing animation fades, results animate in

### RESULTS

What the user sees (top to bottom):

**Alert banner** at the very top:
"🔔 Change detected — DCRB total fund value reached new high of $14.1B (up from $13.2B). Calendar year 2025 net return: 14.1%"
Subtle, elegant highlight — not a screaming red box.

**Summary stat cards** (row of 4, numbers count up from zero on load):
- "31 metrics extracted" / subtitle: "from 4 documents"
- "12 commitments found" / subtitle: "~$1.8B total"
- "4 intelligence signals"
- "4 pension funds scanned"

**Intelligence signals** (cards with colored left border, slide in):
1. "Multi-LP Signal — Stonepeak received $323M from NY State CRF (fund + co-invest) to Global Renewables Fund II. Watch for other LPs committing."
2. "Large Termination — NY State CRF terminated T. Rowe Price ($2B). Capital moved to cash. Reallocation signal."
3. "New High — DCRB fund hit $14.1B all-time high. CY2025 return of 14.1%."
4. "Infrastructure Secondaries — NJ DOI proposing $300M to Ardian ASF IX. Would be largest dedicated infra secondaries fund at $7.5B target."

**Filters** above the table:
- Dropdown: Metric type (All, Commitment, Termination, Performance, Fee Structure, AUM/NAV, Co-Investment)
- Dropdown: Asset class (All, Infrastructure, Real Assets, Private Equity, Credit, Public Equities, Natural Resources, Total Fund)
- Result count: "Showing X of Y metrics"
- Buttons: "Save Search" and "Export"

**Results table** (sortable by clicking column headers, rows stagger in on load):
Columns: Date | LP | Fund | GP/Manager | Metric (colored badge) | Value | Asset Class | Source | Pg

**Evidence panel** (when user clicks any row, accordion expands below):
- Left: metadata grid (LP, Fund, GP, Strategy, Currency, Page, Confidence)
- Right: quote block showing the exact source text with the key value highlighted in bold
- Source document filename below the quote

### DASHBOARD

Bloomberg-style analytics view. Four chart cards:

1. **Commitments by asset class** — donut or bar chart
   Data: Infrastructure (~$1.1B), Real Assets (~$323M), Private Equity (~$113M), Credit ($200M), Natural Resources ($100M), Public Equities ($2B termination)

2. **Commitment activity over time** — bar chart by month
   Data: Nov 2025 (heavy — NY CRF activity), Dec 2025 (DCRB closings), Jan 2026 (NJ DOI + DCRB)

3. **Top GPs by capital committed** — horizontal bar chart
   Data: Stonepeak ($323M), CVC DIF ($500M), Ardian ($300M), Blackrock/Kreos ($200M), DCRB Fund AN ($150M), Quantum ($100M), Updata ($100M)

4. **LP activity overview** — cards or bar chart
   Data: NY State CRF (7 transactions), DCRB (4 transactions), NJ DOI (3 transactions), Santa Barbara ERS (2 commitments + performance data)

### TRACKERS

Pre-populated list of saved trackers (demo data):

1. "Infra commitments — top US pension funds" — Active — 4 sources — 12 metrics — Last match: today — Weekly
2. "Manager terminations Q4 2025" — Active — 6 sources — 3 metrics — Last match: 2 days ago — Daily
3. "PE fund performance tracking" — Active — 3 sources — 8 metrics — Last match: 5 days ago — Monthly
4. "NJ DOI new investments" — Paused — 1 source — 3 metrics — Last match: 12 days ago — Weekly

Each tracker is a card with: name, status badge (Active green / Paused yellow), source count, metric count, last match date, frequency badge. Three-dot menu for pause/edit/delete.

### UPLOAD

A drag-and-drop zone for PDF files. Clean and simple.

When a file is dropped (for the demo, this can just show a simulated extraction):
- File name and size appear with a checkmark animation
- "Extract" button appears
- On click: same processing animation as the search flow
- Then: results appear in the same table format

For a REAL working version: this page sends the PDF as base64 to the Anthropic Messages API, uses the system prompt from the SYSTEM PROMPT section below, and renders the structured JSON response.

## SYSTEM PROMPT (for live extraction via Upload page)

Use this when sending a PDF to the Anthropic API for real extraction:

```
You are a financial data extraction agent specialized in US public pension fund documents.

You will receive a PDF document from a public pension fund (board meeting minutes, transaction reports, investment memos, performance reports, IPC reports).

Extract ALL financial metrics into structured JSON. Be thorough — extract every single data point.

Return ONLY valid JSON (no markdown fences, no explanation, no preamble) with this structure:

{
  "document_metadata": {
    "source_organization": "string",
    "document_type": "string",
    "document_date": "YYYY-MM-DD",
    "reporting_period": "string"
  },
  "extracted_metrics": [
    {
      "date": "YYYY-MM-DD",
      "lp_name": "string",
      "fund_name": "string",
      "gp_manager": "string",
      "metric_type": "Commitment | Termination | NAV | IRR | TVPI | DPI | AUM | Management Fee | Carry | Target Fund Size | Target Return | Asset Allocation | Co-Investment | Distribution | Capital Call",
      "value": "string — preserve original format",
      "currency": "USD | EUR | GBP",
      "asset_class": "string",
      "strategy": "string",
      "page_reference": "number or null",
      "evidence_text": "exact sentence from document, max 150 chars",
      "confidence": "high | medium | low"
    }
  ],
  "cross_reference_signals": [
    {
      "signal_type": "string",
      "description": "string"
    }
  ]
}

Rules:
1. Extract EVERY commitment, termination, allocation, performance metric, fee structure
2. Fee structures: separate entries for mgmt fee AND carry
3. Performance: separate entries for IRR, TVPI, DPI per fund
4. Always include evidence_text
5. "No activity" sections: note with value "No activity"
6. Proposed investments: use Commitment but note "proposed" in evidence
7. Co-investments: separate entries from main fund commitments
8. Capture target fund size and target returns
```

API call details:
- Endpoint: https://api.anthropic.com/v1/messages
- Model: claude-sonnet-4-20250514
- Max tokens: 8000
- Send PDF as base64 document content block
